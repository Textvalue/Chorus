# Penkala + Company Legacy — 3-Minute Hackathon Pitch (5 slides max)

**The brands:** **Penkala** (GTM) and **Company Legacy** (manufacturing) — two products, one context engine underneath.

**The one-liner:** *Every company is sitting on a second brain it can't access. We turn scattered company knowledge into decisions — and we're already doing it in two industries.*

**Total time: 3:00.** Speak to the slide, don't read it. 5 slides.

---

## Slide 1 — Hook + problem (0:00–0:35)

**On screen:**
> "Your company already knows the answer. It just can't remember where it wrote it down."

Below: three icons — CRM, a stack of PDFs, a folder of Excels — pointing into a void. Label: *"Where company knowledge goes to die."*

**Say:**
"Every company generates thousands of documents, calls, and spreadsheets. The knowledge is all there — buried in files nobody opens again. Companies don't have a data problem; they have a context problem. Every decision starts from zero, and AI tools fail because they're guessing without your context."

---

## Slide 2 — What we built + Penkala (0:35–1:15)

**On screen:** Center node labeled **the company's second brain (context engine)**. Arrows in: *features, competitors, ICPs, client calls, demos, CRM, marketing data, PDFs, Excels.* Arrow out: *better decisions.* Beside it: **Penkala** screenshot — a LinkedIn carousel.

**Say:**
"So we built a self-evolving brain for your company. It learns from everything — product, competitors, customer conversations, CRM, your files — and gets smarter the more it ingests. Our first product, **Penkala**, puts that to work for go-to-market: it writes LinkedIn posts, images, and carousels that actually sound like you, grounded in your real positioning. It's live."

## Slide 3 — The pivot: Company Legacy (1:15–2:05)

**On screen:** Timeline: *Posted Penkala on LinkedIn → his dad at Bunge reaches out → "same problem, but in manufacturing"* → **Company Legacy.** Pull quote: *"2,000+ Excel files of experiments — all that learning just sits there."*

**Say:**
"Then something happened. A teammate posted Penkala on LinkedIn. His father — who works at Bunge, a global agribusiness — messaged him: 'We have your exact problem. 2,000-plus Excel files of experiments, and all that learning just sits there.'

That's when it clicked: 80% of what we built isn't about marketing — it's about context. So we built our second product, **Company Legacy**, for manufacturing. And it's not just Bunge: a manufacturer in Croatia told us their AI estimates kept failing — no salary data, no team speed, no view of other projects. Same root problem, same fix."

Make the screenshots carry it. On Slide 4:

1. **Penkala** — input (company context) → output (a polished LinkedIn carousel). "Context in, branded content out."

## Anticipated questions (have these ready)

**"Isn't this just RAG / a vector database?"**
RAG retrieves chunks. We build and maintain an evolving model of the company — entities, relationships, what's current vs. stale — and apply it to specific jobs. Retrieval is easy; context modeling and keeping it fresh is the moat.

**"Why will you win vs. ChatGPT/Copilot?"**
Horizontal assistants have no durable company context. We're the layer that makes them accurate for one company. We sit on top of them, not against them.

**"Why two brands instead of one?"**
The pain and the buyer look totally different in GTM vs. manufacturing, so each brand speaks its market's language. The engine underneath is shared — build context once, monetize it twice.

**"What's the wedge / who do you sell to first?"**
Two beachheads already pulling us in: GTM teams (fast, self-serve, content ROI is obvious) and manufacturing/ops teams sitting on dead Excel archives (high pain, high willingness to pay).

**"How do you make money?"**
Per-seat or per-workspace SaaS, priced on the value of the decision — content is cheap to prove, estimation/R&D context is high-ticket.

**"Data security?"**
Context stays within the customer's workspace; the brain is per-company, never shared across tenants. (Confirm this matches your actual architecture before saying it.)

**"What did you build at this hackathon specifically?"**
Two deployed products — Penkala and Company Legacy — on one shared context engine, plus the ingestion pipeline that turns files/calls/CRM into usable company context.

---

## Delivery tips

- The pivot story (Slide 3) is your strongest moment — slow down there. It proves market *pull*, not push.
- Say the number out loud: **"2,000+ Excel files."** Specifics sell.
- "Build context once, monetize twice" is your best investor line — use it on Slide 4 even if unasked.
- Don't explain architecture in 3 minutes. Sell the pattern: lost context is universal, we fix it, here's proof.
- End on the ask. Judges remember the last sentence.
